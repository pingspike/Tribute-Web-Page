Abraham Lincoln

16th president of the United States from 1861 to 1865, issuer of the emancipation proclamation that declared freedom for slaves within the Confederacy in 1863, and maintainer of a nation

Those who deny freedom to others, deserve it not for themselves...

- Abraham Lincoln

Notable Events During his Time

1809 Abraham Lincoln is born in a one-room log cabin at a farm in Kentucky, USA.
1824 Lincoln, now 15, farms, and does work-for-hire for neighbors. He attends school in the fall and winter after the harvest, while borrowing books to read whenever possible.
1832 Lincoln runs for the Illinois General Assembly and loses. Also, the Black Hawk War breaks out, and Lincoln forms a Regiment of the Illinois Militia with his neighbors. He saw no military action during his months of service.
1834 Lincoln runs for public office again at the age of 24 and is elected to the Illinois General Assembly. He begins to study law, using borrowed books. Lincoln takes a seat in the state government.
1836 Lincoln is re-elected to the Illinois General Assembly and receives his license to practice law.
1837 Lincoln is admitted to the Illinois Bar.
1842 Lincoln and Mary Todd marry.
1843 Lincoln's first son is born, Robert Todd Lincoln.
1846 Lincoln's second son is born, Edward "Eddie" Baker Lincoln. Lincoln is elected to U.S. Congress.
1847–1848 He is a U.S. Congressman and becomes known for opposing slavery.
1849 Lincoln fails to be appointed commissioner of the General Land Office and returns to Springfield, leaving politics to practice law. He is also granted a patent for buoying vessels over shoals, and he is the only president ever granted a patent.
1850 His son, Edward Lincoln dies before his fourth birthday, of possibly tuberculosis. Lincoln resumes his travels in the Judicial Circuit. His wife gives birth to another son, William "Willie" Wallace Lincoln.
1853 Thomas "Tad" Lincoln is born.
1855 Lincoln loses an election for U.S. Senator. At this time, senators were chosen by the Illinois House of Representatives, and not by direct election.
1856 Lincoln helps organize the new Republican Party of Illinois and at the first Republican convention, Lincoln gets 110 votes for the vice-presidential nomination where he gains national attention but loses the nomination.
1858 Lincoln receives the Republican nomination for Senator from Illinois. He gives his House Divided speech at the state convention in Springfield. He and Douglas also engage in a series of seven debates known today as the Lincoln-Douglas Debates.
1859 The Illinois legislature elects Douglas for the U.S. Senate over Lincoln. In the fall, Lincoln makes his last trip through the Judicial Circuit.
1860 Lincoln is elected as the 16th President of the United States, receiving 180 of 303 electoral votes and about 40 percent of the popular vote in a five-way election. He is the first Republican President. On December 20, 1860, South Carolina secedes from the Union, followed within two months by Mississippi, Florida, Alabama, Georgia, Louisiana, and Texas.
1861 On April 12 Confederate artillery opens fire on Fort Sumter in Charleston Harbor. President Lincoln calls for 75,000 volunteers to serve three months in the Union army. The Civil War has begun. On July 21, , the Union Army suffers a humiliating defeat at the First Battle of Bull Run. The President realizes the war will be long.
1862 William Lincoln dies at age 11 of typhus. Mary Todd Lincoln is devastated and never fully recovers. April 16, Lincoln signs an act that abolishes slavery in the District of Columbia. On September 22, the President issues the preliminary Emancipation Proclamation. December 31, the President signs a bill admitting West Virginia to the Union as the 35th state.
1863 On January 1, President Lincoln issues the final Emancipation Proclamation freeing all slaves in territories held by Confederates. On July 3, the Battle of Gettysburg ends and the Confederate defeat is a turning point of the cival war. On November 19, Lincoln delivers the Gettysburg Address at a ceremony dedicating the battlefield as a National Cemetery. Though not well received at the time, it will take its place among the most famous speeches in history.
1864 Lincoln is re-elected, defeating Democrat George B. McClellan. Lincoln gets 212 of 233 electoral votes and 55 percent of the popular vote.
1865 General Robert E. Lee surrenders his Confederate Army to General Ulysses S. Grant following the Battle of Appomattox Court House in Virginia. On April 11, Lincoln makes his last public speech, which focuses on the problems of reconstruction. On April 14, Lincoln and his wife, Mary, see the play Our American Cousin at Ford’s Theater. About 10:13 p.m., during the third act of the play, John Wilkes Booth shoots the 56-year old president in the head. Doctors attend to the president in the theater then move him to a house across the street. He never regains consciousness and dies at 7:22 the following the morning. On December 6, the Thirteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution, passed by Congress on January 31, 1865, is finally ratified and slavery is abolished.

I leave you, hoping that the lamp of liberty will burn in your bosoms until there shall no longer be a doubt that all men are created free and equal.

- Abraham Lincoln
